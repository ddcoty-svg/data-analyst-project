# Customer Segmentation & Spending Behavior Analysis

## 1. Project Overview  
This project analyzes mall customer data to understand spending behavior and segment customers based on income and spending patterns. The goal is to identify high-value customers and uncover insights that can drive targeted marketing and business strategies.  

---

## 2. Objectives  
a. Identify top-spending customers  
b. Analyze the relationship between income and spending behavior  
c. Segment customers into meaningful groups  
d. Evaluate contribution of top customers to overall spending  

---

## 3. Tools and Skills Used  
a. Python (Pandas, Matplotlib)  
b. Data Cleaning and Preprocessing  
c. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)  
d. Customer Segmentation Logic  
e. Data Visualization  

---

## 4. Data Source  
a. Mall Customers dataset (CSV file)  
b. Contains customer demographics such as income and spending score  

---

## 5. Key Analysis and Visuals  

### a. Top Customer Analysis  
Identifies the top 10 customers based on spending score to highlight high-value individuals.  

### b. Pareto Analysis (80/20 Rule)  
Evaluates whether the top 20% of customers contribute a significant portion of total spending.  

### c. Customer Segmentation  
Customers are divided into segments based on income and spending behavior:  
- High Value  
- Potential  
- Impulsive  
- Low Value  

### d. Segment-wise Analysis  
Analyzes average income and spending score for each segment to understand behavioral patterns.  

### e. Income vs Spending Visualization  
Bar chart showing average spending score across different income groups to identify trends.  

---

## 6. Problem Statement  
a. Businesses lack clarity on which customers generate the most value  
b. Difficulty in targeting the right customer segments for marketing  
c. Limited understanding of how income influences spending behavior  

---

## 7. Key Insights  
a. A small percentage of customers contributes significantly to overall spending  
b. High-income customers do not always have high spending scores  
c. Distinct customer segments show different behavioral patterns  
d. Some low-income customers exhibit high spending tendencies (impulsive buyers)  

---

## 8. Business Impact  
a. Helps businesses target high-value customers more effectively  
b. Enables personalized marketing strategies for different segments  
c. Improves customer retention by focusing on valuable segments  
d. Supports better decision-making in promotions and pricing strategies  

---

## 9. Conclusion  
This project demonstrates how customer segmentation and spending analysis can provide actionable insights for businesses. By understanding different customer groups, companies can optimize their marketing efforts and improve overall profitability.
